Introduction

Purpose of the Guide

This guide has been created to provide a clear understanding of how the Workgroup for Resilient Industrial Systems (WRIS) operates. It consolidates essential information about the organization’s structure, expectations, processes, and resources into a single reference. By following this guide, members can quickly become familiar with the workflows, responsibilities, and opportunities available.

Mission of WRIS

The WRIS is dedicated to enabling top students to fully demonstrate their talent and abilities within a scope of study that is modern, and industry relevant. Our mission is to study, analyze, and share knowledge that can prevent failures, mitigate risks, and promote sustainable operations across industries. Members engage in research, collaboration, and problem-solving initiatives that collectively strengthen the reliability and adaptability of industrial infrastructures. By joining WRIS, members become part of a community focused on bridging theoretical insights with practical applications that have real-world impact.

Vision & Values

WRIS operates on a set of core principles that guide decision-making and conduct. We value integrity, collaboration, and continuous learning, ensuring that all members act ethically and contribute positively to the group’s objectives. Innovation and critical thinking are encouraged, empowering members to explore new solutions while respecting the knowledge and experience of peers. Our vision is to cultivate a community where members not only develop professionally but also contribute to building resilient, adaptive industrial systems that can withstand challenges and thrive in an uncertain world.
