Policies & Compliance

Security & Privacy

Protecting organizational data and member information is a top priority at WRIS. All members are required to follow established security protocols when handling sensitive data, accessing internal systems, and sharing materials. Passwords, access credentials, and confidential project information must be stored securely, with strict adherence to role-based access policies. Unauthorized sharing of data, whether internal or external, is prohibited. Members are trained on proper data management, secure communication methods, and procedures for reporting potential security breaches. Compliance with these policies ensures the integrity of organizational operations and the safety of member contributions.

Intellectual Property

Contributors retain full ownership and authorship of their original work produced within WRIS. By contributing, members grant WRIS a non-exclusive, perpetual right to publish, host, and distribute submitted work as open-content through public GitHub repositories or other open platforms. This grant does not restrict contributors from reusing, modifying, publishing, or commercializing their own work independently, including for paid or proprietary uses, provided appropriate attribution to the original WRIS project is maintained.

Ethical Guidelines

WRIS is committed to upholding the highest ethical standards in research, project development, and professional conduct. Members are expected to adhere to applicable industry standards, academic integrity principles, and research ethics policies. This includes ensuring accuracy in reporting results, avoiding conflicts of interest, respecting confidentiality, and conducting work with transparency and accountability. Ethical adherence supports the credibility of WRIS outputs, fosters trust among stakeholders, and cultivates a professional environment where members can confidently collaborate and innovate.
