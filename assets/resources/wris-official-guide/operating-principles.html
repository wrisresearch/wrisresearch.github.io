5. Operating Principles

Collaboration & Communication

Effective collaboration is central to WRISâ€™s mission. Members are expected to participate in scheduled meetings punctually, contribute actively, and maintain professional etiquette at all times. Communication should be clear, concise, and documented when necessary, ensuring that team members and leadership have a shared understanding of ongoing work. Decision-making follows a transparent process: proposals are discussed in relevant channels, reviewed by assigned leads, and finalized with documented outcomes. This approach ensures accountability, fosters constructive debate, and encourages input from all levels of the organization. Active listening and respectful dialogue are required in all interactions, promoting a collaborative culture where ideas are evaluated on merit rather than rank.

Project Standards & Workflows

The executive council develops assignments based on necessity and industry trends. Members are fully responsible for initiative, creativity, and management within their chosen assignments. However, deliverables must meet all criteria specifically stipulated by the post on the workboard. Stipulations may include deadlines, dependencies, expected outputs, and more.
