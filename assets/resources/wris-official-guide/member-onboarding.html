Member Onboarding

Getting Started Checklist

After registering on the Members page you will have Subscriber status. These new members recieve a welcome email with a basic procedures on how to contribute. First, you will have to use your official name on internal communication and platforms (Google, Microsoft, etc.). Through our website, you will see resources such as the workboard become available.

Orientation Sessions

Orientation is conducted through structured sessions designed to introduce members to WRIS’s workflows, objectives, and culture. New members should review the schedule of live and recorded sessions, including tutorials on tools, project management, and reporting standards. Recommended materials, such as handbooks and example deliverables, are provided to reinforce the concepts discussed. These sessions help members understand expectations, learn best practices, and begin forming connections with peers and mentors.

Code of Conduct

WRIS emphasizes professionalism, collaboration, and mutual respect. Members are expected to engage constructively in discussions, provide and accept feedback gracefully, and communicate clearly and respectfully. Collaborative work must be conducted transparently, with all contributions properly documented and attributed. Adherence to these principles ensures a supportive environment where members can learn, innovate, and produce high-quality work while upholding the organization’s values.
