Organization Overview

History & Background

The Workgroup for Resilient Industrial Systems (WRIS) was founded by students of Athabasca University to address the absence of structured interdisciplinary academic and industrial collaboration. Recognizing the common challenges students face—such as limited access to real-world work experience and direction toward pressing industrial issues.

Structure

WRIS operates through a structured framework designed to organize projects, responsibilities, and growth. The executive council posts projects on a workboard. Members can select projects aligned with their interests. Progress and contributions are tracked, and members advance through a tiered system by completing a set number and type of projects—such as research papers, video lectures, or reports. This structured approach ensures transparent pathways for recognition and promotion within the organization.

Membership Types

Membership in WRIS is tiered to reflect engagement, responsibility, and contribution. Members advance through the following levels by completing specific work quotas:

Subscribers: Access resources, follow updates, and attend events. No work quota required.
Juniors: Complete 3 minor projects (e.g., research notes, brief reports, or recorded discussions) or 1 significant project (e.g., full reports, tutorials, or presentations).
Core: Complete 3 significant projects (e.g., full reports, tutorials, or presentations).
Seniors: Complete 5 significant projects and mentor at least 1 junior member.
Executive Members: Promotion is granted after completing multiple major projects. Executives help guide the group’s direction, review work produced by members, and manage core operational duties such as administrative coordination, documentation, and general organizational support.
