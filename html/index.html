About Our Workgroup

The Workgroup for Resilient Industrial Systems is a student-led initiative in which members first focus on acquiring a deep understanding of industrial and infrastructure systems, including supply chains, energy grids, manufacturing plants, and networks, and how these systems fail and recover under stress. Once they have internalized these concepts through collaborative learning, computational modeling, and small-scale simulations, members produce teaching materials such as slides, video walkthroughs, guided readings, and virtual lectures to educate other students. Through this process, members develop high-value skills including research and publishing by creating and documenting knowledge, public speaking through presenting content to others, project management by coordinating workflows and deadlines, business analysis by evaluating systems and dependencies, data and analytics by modeling and visualizing system behaviors, and media production by crafting clear, engaging educational content. Membership is structured to recognize growing contribution and leadership, beginning with junior members who build foundational knowledge, progressing to members who actively produce teaching content, senior members who oversee quality and peer review, and executive members who coordinate projects and manage overall workgroup activities.

Activities focus on modeling system dependencies, simulating failure scenarios, analyzing real-world industrial case studies, and producing teaching media that translate complex systems into accessible formats for other students. Contributions are tracked in shared repositories, project management tools, and verified with digital certificates, ensuring each member’s learning and output is publicly demonstrable. This approach allows members to develop transferable, high-impact skills while simultaneously producing resources that benefit the broader student community. The workgroup is especially suited to freshmen because it combines skill-building, applied computational tools, and tangible outputs in a collaborative environment, laying a foundation for leadership, analysis, and advanced projects in future semesters.
